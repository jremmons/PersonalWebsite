D. Basu, R. M. Arthur, Y. Guo and J. W. Trobaugh, "3D Temperature Imaging during Non-Uniform Heating using Ultrasonic Backscattered Energy", IEEE Transactions on Ultrasonics, Ferroelectrics, and Frequency Control, in review.

Thermal therapies to treat pathological tissue at therapeutic levels while minimizing damage to adjacent normal tissue benefit from monitoring volumetric temperature distributions during heating. Ultrasound is a non-invasive thermal imaging modality that is non-ionizing, inexpensive, portable, and simple-to-use. Our group has been using the change in backscattered energy (CBE) for monitoring temperature in soft tissue. To test the efficacy of CBE as a temperature estimator during non-uniform tissue heating, we obtained 3D phase-array tissue images with temperature verified using thermocouple grids.

Previously determined temperature dependence of CBE was used to estimate temperature distributions in 2D images from 8 specimens of turkey breast in a 37oC water bath heated from a localized high-temperature source. Error in CBE-based temperature estimates was about 1oC. These experimental results were consistent with our theoretical and simulation studies, as well as previous experiments with uniform heating. Success of CBE temperature estimation for non-uniform heating supports the clinical application of non-invasive temperature estimation using ultrasound for both hyperthermia and ablation thermal therapies.
